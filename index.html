About Us

Aarambh’s major objective is to provide special service to individuals with autism from varied economic strata.
The incidence of autism is rapidly increasing from 2000 till date and might still go up. (Please refer http://www.cdc.gov/ncbddd/autism/data.html ) Autism is a special Condition where the individuals affected need help in a multidisciplinary approach for their progress.
In India, services for individuals with autism are meagre and are priced at a very high cost making them unaffordable for a middle class family.
Autism is not always associated with mental retardation and hence the ability to learn could be facilitated through appropriate teaching methods viz. special education, occupational therapy, speech therapy, medical intervention and training to improve social skills. This requirement is almost for a life time.

About Autism

Autism is a life long developmental disorder which typically appears during the first three years of life. Autism affects the way the brain processes the information, and prevents the individuals from properly understanding what they see, hear and otherwise sense. Children with autism do not follow the same rules of communication and socialisation that comes naturally to other children. Symptoms range from a mild learning and social disability to a severe impairment, so known as “spectrum” disorder.
Autism spectrum disorder (ASD) and autism are both general terms for a group of complex disorders of brain development. These disorders are characterized, in varying degrees, by difficulties in social interaction, verbal and nonverbal communication and repetitive behaviors.
With the May 2013 publication of the DSM-5 diagnostic manual, all autism disorders were merged into one umbrella diagnosis of ASD. Previously, they were recognized as distinct subtypes, including autistic disorder, childhood disintegrative disorder, pervasive developmental disorder-not otherwise specified (PDD-NOS) and Asperger syndrome.

ASD can be associated with intellectual disability, difficulties in motor coordination and attention and physical health issues such as sleep and gastrointestinal disturbances. Some persons with ASD excel in visual skills, music, math and art.

Services & Support

  • Children & Adult

    Aakanksha

    'Aakanksha', a day school in which children from 3-18 years will be grouped in the class rooms based on their needs and abilities. The school combines group activities and one-on- one teaching, as well as speech therapy and occupational therapy. In addition to building upon academic skills.

    Aasra

    'Aasra', an hourly intervention program for the child with our trained staff on one-on- one basis. The methods are based on behavior modification and structured teaching (TEACCH). Elements of TEACCH, Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA), various form of Alternative and Augmentative communication (AAC) are a part of methods used.

    Srishti

    'Srishti',a vocational training center for young persons with Autism. Students are given training to make utilitarian and beautiful handmade items such as paper bags, decorative, book markers, envelopes, gift bags, saree painting, block printing, pen stands, calendars, paper plates, gardening, etc.,

    Aathmiya

    'Aathmiya', a family counseling program to family members in a friendly environment. Parents are free to Discuss any issues related to pressure and stress of parenting a child with Autism.
    Counselled 700 & above of families having children with autism

    •                              Signs of autism

    Research now suggests that children as young as 1 year old can show signs of autism. The most important thing you can do as a parent or caregiver is to learn the early signs of autism and understand the typical developmental milestones your child should be reaching at different ages. Please look over the following list. If you have any concerns about your child's development, don't wait. Speak to your doctor about screening your child for autism. While validated screening for autism starts only as young as 18 months, the best bet for younger children is to have their development screened at every well visit with a highly validated developmental screening tool. If your child does have autism, early intervention may be his or her best hope.


    *This information has been provided by First Signs, Inc. ©2001-2005
  • No big smiles or other warm, joyful expressions by six months or thereafter.

  • No back-and-forth sharing of sounds, smiles, or other facial expressions by nine months or thereafter.

  • No babbling by 12 months.

  • No back-and-forth gestures, such as pointing, showing, reaching, or waving by 12 months.

  • No words by 16 months

  • No two-word meaningful phrases (without imitating or repeating) by 24 months.

  • Any loss of speech or babbling or social skills at any age.

    •                      Other Signs of Autism

  • Does not respond to his/her name

  • Avoids eye contact.

  • Repeats or echoes words, questions, and has meaningless self talk.

  • Does not point with index finger to indicate interest.

  • Difficulty in mixing and playing with other children.

  • Stereotype , repetitive behaviours like flapping hands, body rocking, aimless running, shouting etc.,

  • Inappropriate laughing & giggling

  • Obsessions to objects

  • Appears to be hyper active

  • Inconsolable crying behaviour

  • Can do some tasks very well (eg.puzzles) but not tasks involving social understanding

  • Display good rote memory for nursery rhymes, commercial jingles.

  • Resistance to changes in routines

  • No understanding of fear and real dangers

  • Not respond to normal teaching methods
