'Aarambh' is initiated by 'Mrs. Sharda Ram' , a mother of a girl child with autism in year 2010 after pursuing her Diploma in Autism Spectrum Disorders and working in the same place to gain experience.
'Aarambh' is a registered not for profit organization that strives for the development and care of individuals with autism.
'Autism' is a neurological disorder that primarily affects socialization and communication skills of person.

Autism appears to have its roots in very early brain development. However, the most obvious signs of autism and symptoms of autism tend to emerge between 2 and 3 years of age. Autism Speaks continues to fund research on effective methods for earlier diagnosis, as early intervention with proven behavioral therapies can improve outcomes. Increasing autism awareness is a key aspect of this work and one in which our families and volunteers play an invaluable role

About Us

Aarambh’s major objective is to provide special service to individuals with autism from varied economic strata. The incidence of autism is rapidly increasing from 2000 till date and might still go up. (Please refer http://www.cdc.gov/ncbddd/autism/data.html ) Autism is a special Condition where the individuals affected need help in a multidisciplinary approach for their progress. In India, services for individuals with autism are meagre and are priced at a very high cost making them unaffordable for a middle class family. Autism is not always associated with mental retardation and hence the ability to learn could be facilitated through appropriate teaching methods viz. special education, occupational therapy, speech therapy, medical intervention and training to improve social skills. This requirement is almost for a life time.

About Autism

Autism spectrum disorder (ASD) and autism are both general terms for a group of complex disorders of brain development. These disorders are characterized, in varying degrees, by difficulties in social interaction, verbal and nonverbal communication and repetitive behaviors. With the May 2013 publication of the DSM-5 diagnostic manual, all autism disorders were merged into one umbrella diagnosis of ASD. Previously, they were recognized as distinct subtypes, including autistic disorder, childhood disintegrative disorder, pervasive developmental disorder-not otherwise specified (PDD-NOS) and Asperger syndrome.

ASD can be associated with intellectual disability, difficulties in motor coordination and attention and physical health issues such as sleep and gastrointestinal disturbances. Some persons with ASD excel in visual skills, music, math and art.

Services & Support

  • Children & Adult

    •

    Aakanksha

    Aakanksha, a day school in which children from 3-18 years will be grouped in the class rooms based on their needs and abilities. The school combines group activities and one-on- one teaching, as well as speech therapy and occupational therapy. In addition to building upon academic skills.

    Aasra

    Aasra, an hourly intervention program for the child with our trained staff on one-on- one basis. The methods are based on behavior modification and structured teaching (TEACCH). Elements of TEACCH, Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA), various form of Alternative and Augmentative communication (AAC) are a part of methods used.

    Srishti

    A vocational training center for young persons with Autism. Students are given training to make utilitarian and beautiful handmade items such as paper bags, decorative, book markers, envelopes, gift bags, saree painting, block printing, pen stands, calendars, paper plates, gardening, etc.,

    Aathmiya

    Aathmiya, a family counseling program to family members in a friendly environment. Parents are free to Discuss any issues related to pressure and stress of parenting a child with Autism.
